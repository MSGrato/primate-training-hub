## Position Summary

The Clinical Veterinary Technician provides comprehensive clinical care for the nonhuman primate (NHP) colony at the WaNPRC under the direction of staff veterinarians. This role is integral to the Veterinary Services group, which oversees preventive medicine, diagnosis and treatment of spontaneous disease, clinical support for research protocols, and overall colony health management. The ideal candidate is passionate about animal welfare, thrives in a collaborative research environment, and brings strong technical skills in veterinary clinical procedures.

## Essential Duties and Responsibilities

### Clinical Care & Diagnostics

- Perform routine health assessments and physical examinations on nonhuman primates under veterinary supervision.
- Collect and process biological samples including blood, urine, cerebrospinal fluid, and tissue specimens for clinical diagnostics and research.
- Administer medications, vaccinations, and treatments as prescribed by attending veterinarians.
- Conduct routine screening tests for tuberculosis and simian viruses, including SRV, STLV, and Macacine alphaherpesvirus (Herpes B virus).
- Interpret diagnostic hematology, clinical chemistry, and microbiology results in support of veterinary decision-making.

### Research Support

- Support research protocols by performing specimen collection, sample processing, and technical procedures in collaboration with research teams.
- Assist with specialized procedures such as timed pregnancies, tethering, endoscopy, and experimental diet administration.
- Maintain accurate and detailed clinical and research records in compliance with institutional and federal requirements.

### Colony Health & Preventive Medicine

- Participate in the preventive medicine program, including scheduling and performing routine wellness exams and screenings.
- Monitor pregnancy evaluations and provide pre- and postnatal care for dams and infants.
- Observe and report behavioral or health changes in colony animals to veterinary staff.

### Compliance & Documentation

- Ensure all activities comply with IACUC-approved protocols, USDA Animal Welfare Act regulations, the Guide for the Care and Use of Laboratory Animals, and institutional policies.
- Maintain detailed medical records and contribute to veterinary reports and documentation.
- Participate in regulatory inspections and accreditation visits (e.g., AAALAC International).

## Required Qualifications

- Associate's degree or higher in Veterinary Technology from an AVMA-accredited program.
- Current Washington State Veterinary Technician license (or eligibility to obtain within 6 months of hire).
- Minimum of 1-2 years of clinical veterinary technician experience.
- Demonstrated proficiency in venipuncture, sample collection, and diagnostic procedures.
- Strong attention to detail with excellent record-keeping skills.
- Ability to safely handle and restrain nonhuman primates.
- Effective communication skills and the ability to work collaboratively in a multidisciplinary team environment.

## Preferred Qualifications

- Credentialed Veterinary Technician (CVT) or Registered Veterinary Technician (RVT) certification.
- Veterinary Technician Specialty (VTS) in Laboratory Animal Medicine or eligibility to pursue.
- Prior experience working with nonhuman primates in a research or clinical setting.
- Experience with diagnostic imaging (radiography, ultrasonography).
- Familiarity with laboratory animal regulatory requirements (USDA, OLAW, AAALAC).
- AALAS certification (ALAT, LAT, or LATG) is a plus.

## Working Conditions

- Work is performed in animal housing facilities and veterinary clinical areas.
- Exposure to nonhuman primates, biological materials, zoonotic agents, and chemical disinfectants.
- Must be able to lift up to 50 pounds and stand for extended periods.
- Enrollment in the Occupational Health and Safety Program is required, including tuberculosis testing and appropriate vaccinations.
- May require occasional weekend, holiday, or on-call availability to ensure continuity of animal care.
