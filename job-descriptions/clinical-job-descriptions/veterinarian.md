## Position Summary

The Staff Veterinarian provides direct clinical and preventive veterinary care for the Center's nonhuman primate colony. Working within the Veterinary Services unit and under the supervision of the Supervisory Veterinarian, this role ensures the health, welfare, and well-being of all primates in compliance with federal regulations, institutional policies, and AAALAC International standards. The position also provides veterinary guidance in support of research protocols reviewed by the Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC).

## Essential Duties and Responsibilities

- Perform routine and emergency clinical examinations, diagnose medical conditions, and develop treatment plans for nonhuman primates, including physical assessments, hematology, microbiology, and serologic testing.
- Administer and oversee the preventive medicine program, including tuberculosis testing and surveillance for simian retrovirus (SRV), simian T-cell leukemia virus (STLV), and Macacine alphaherpesvirus (Herpes B virus).
- Provide pre-surgical, surgical, and post-operative care; assist with performance of a wide range of survival and terminal surgical procedures in dedicated surgical facilities.
- Review and provide veterinary input on IACUC research protocols to ensure compliance with animal welfare regulations and best practices.
- Conduct pregnancy evaluations and deliver pre- and postnatal care for dams and infants within the breeding colony.
- Collaborate with behavioral management staff to support environmental enrichment programs and the psychological well-being of housed primates.
- Maintain accurate, detailed medical records and contribute to colony health databases.
- Provide professional veterinary consultation to research investigators, animal care staff, and Center leadership.
- Participate in on-call emergency coverage on a rotating basis, including nights, weekends, and holidays.
- Support training and mentorship of veterinary technicians, externs, and laboratory animal medicine residents.

## Required Qualifications

- Doctor of Veterinary Medicine (DVM) or equivalent degree from an AVMA-accredited institution.
- Valid veterinary license or eligibility for licensure in the State of Washington.
- USDA Category I accreditation (or eligibility to obtain).
