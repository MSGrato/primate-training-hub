## Position Summary

The Surgical Veterinary Technician provides specialized anesthesia and surgical support for the nonhuman primate (NHP) colony at the WaNPRC under the direction of staff veterinarians. This role is integral to the Veterinary Services group, delivering expert perioperative care for both clinical and research surgical procedures. The ideal candidate is passionate about animal welfare, highly skilled in anesthesia and surgical assisting, and thrives in a collaborative biomedical research environment.

## Essential Duties and Responsibilities

### Anesthesia & Perioperative Care

- Perform anesthetic induction, intubation, and maintenance for nonhuman primates undergoing clinical and research procedures.
- Monitor anesthetic depth and physiological parameters including heart rate, respiratory rate, blood pressure, SpO2, ETCO2, and temperature throughout procedures.
- Manage anesthetic recovery, including extubation, pain assessment, and post-anesthetic monitoring until patients are stable.
- Calculate and prepare anesthetic drug protocols as directed by veterinarians, maintaining accurate controlled substance logs.
- Troubleshoot anesthetic emergencies and assist with cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) as needed.

### Surgical Support

- Prepare surgical suites, including setup of instruments, drapes, equipment, and sterile supplies in accordance with aseptic protocols.
- Assist veterinarians during surgical procedures as a scrub or circulating technician.
- Perform instrument sterilization, wrapping, and quality assurance of autoclaved packs.
- Monitor patients intraoperatively, including management of IV fluids, ventilator settings, and physiologic parameters.
- Provide postoperative care and monitoring, including wound management, bandage changes, and administration of prescribed analgesics and antibiotics.

### Diagnostic Imaging

- Perform radiography, ultrasonography, and other diagnostic imaging procedures under veterinary direction.
- Position patients appropriately for imaging studies while maintaining anesthetic stability.
- Process and archive diagnostic images and assist with interpretation as directed.

### Research Support

- Provide anesthesia and surgical support for IACUC-approved research protocols in collaboration with research teams.
- Assist with specialized procedures such as implant surgeries, catheter placements, biopsies, and endoscopy.
- Maintain accurate and detailed surgical, anesthesia, and research records in compliance with institutional and federal requirements.

### Compliance & Documentation

- Ensure all activities comply with IACUC-approved protocols, USDA Animal Welfare Act regulations, the Guide for the Care and Use of Laboratory Animals, and institutional policies.
- Maintain detailed anesthesia and surgical records and contribute to veterinary reports and documentation.
- Participate in regulatory inspections and accreditation visits (e.g., AAALAC International).
- Maintain surgical equipment, anesthesia machines, and monitoring devices, coordinating maintenance and calibration as needed.

## Required Qualifications

- Associate's degree or higher in Veterinary Technology from an AVMA-accredited program.
- Current Washington State Veterinary Technician license (or eligibility to obtain within 6 months of hire).
- Minimum of 2-3 years of veterinary technician experience with a focus on anesthesia and surgical support.
- Demonstrated proficiency in anesthetic induction, monitoring, and recovery in small or large animal species.
- Knowledge of aseptic technique, surgical instrumentation, and sterilization procedures.
- Experience with patient monitoring equipment (pulse oximetry, capnography, blood pressure, ECG).
- Strong attention to detail with excellent record-keeping skills.
- Ability to safely handle and restrain nonhuman primates.
- Effective communication skills and the ability to work collaboratively in a multidisciplinary team environment.

## Preferred Qualifications

- Credentialed Veterinary Technician (CVT) or Registered Veterinary Technician (RVT) certification.
- Veterinary Technician Specialty (VTS) in Anesthesia and Analgesia, or Surgery, or eligibility to pursue.
- Prior experience working with nonhuman primates in a research or clinical setting.
- Experience with diagnostic imaging (radiography, ultrasonography).
- Experience with mechanical ventilation and advanced anesthetic monitoring.
- Familiarity with laboratory animal regulatory requirements (USDA, OLAW, AAALAC).
- AALAS certification (ALAT, LAT, or LATG) is a plus.

## Working Conditions

- Work is performed primarily in surgical suites, veterinary clinical areas, and animal housing facilities.
- Exposure to nonhuman primates, biological materials, zoonotic agents, chemical disinfectants, and anesthetic gases.
- Must be able to lift up to 50 pounds, stand for extended periods, and maintain focus during lengthy surgical procedures.
- Enrollment in the Occupational Health and Safety Program is required, including tuberculosis testing and appropriate vaccinations.
- May require occasional weekend, holiday, or on-call availability to ensure continuity of animal care and surgical support.
