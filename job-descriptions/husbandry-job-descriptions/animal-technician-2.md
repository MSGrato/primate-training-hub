# Animal Technician 2

- Last updated: May 2, 2025

## UW Code and Representative Group

- 17725 (NE S SEIU 925 Non Supv)
- 22411 (NE H NI SEIU 925 Non Supv)

## Class Series Concept

Perform animal care duties; provide care for animals and animal facilities.

## Basic Function

Independently perform routine husbandry tasks involved in the care and feeding of animals, and maintenance of animal facilities

## Distinguishing Characteristics

Perform basic preventive animal health maintenance duties and select administrative functions. Independently perform routine and advanced animal care duties.

## Typical Work

- Perform duties of Animal Technician 1;
- Restrain animals for examination or injection; collect urine and/or fecal samples as directed;
- Perform preventive animal health care duties;
- Collect and evaluate water and environmental samples to record water quality, and support animal health and sanitation efficacy testing;
- Assist in the installation and routine maintenance of caging systems and other non-fixed facility equipment;
- Document equipment maintenance and repairs in accordance with facility guidelines;
- Perform delegated administrative functions in support of animal facility management and operations;
- May administer oral or topical medications;
- May collect basic examination information such as temperature, pulse, respiration, weight; record as needed;
- May assist in clinical or research experiment activities;
- May assist in training and directing the work of new and lower level animal technicians and research technicians;
- May euthanize laboratory animals;
- Perform other duties as assigned.

## Minimum Qualifications

American Association for Laboratory Animal Science (AALAS) Assistant Laboratory Animal Technician (ALAT) certification.

One year of experience as an Animal Technician 1 or equivalent.

Equivalent education and/or experience may substitute for minimum qualifications except when there are legal requirements, such as a license, certification, and/or registration.
